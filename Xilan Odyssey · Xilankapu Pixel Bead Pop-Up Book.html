Cultural Origins

What is Xilankapu?

Xilankapu — meaning "earth-flower coverlet" in the Tujia language — is a traditional brocade weaving craft passed down through generations of the Tujia people in western Hunan and western Hubei. In 2006, it was inscribed on China's first National Intangible Cultural Heritage list. Renowned for its vivid colours and intricate geometric patterns, it carries deep Tujia sentiment toward nature, ancestors, and life itself.

// History & Weaving Technique

Xilankapu traces its origins to the Qin-Han period, with a history of over two thousand years. Legend holds that Xilan, a Tujia weaving girl, gave her life to protect the secrets of the craft — imbuing it with a sacred, mythic dimension.

Weavers employ the "continuous warp, broken weft" pick-up technique, building geometric motifs in vivid colour against a black or deep-blue ground. A single fine piece may take months or even years to complete.

Motifs span flora, fauna, natural phenomena, and abstract geometry. Classic patterns include the "Oriole Flower," "Terrace Flower," and "Eight Hooks" — each bearing its own name and meaning, forming the most important unwritten chapter of Tujia history.

Qin–Han
Origins — Tujia ancestors weave geometric patterns on wooden looms; the first forms of Xilankapu emerge
Tang–Song
Golden Age — The brocade tradition matures; "Tujia Silk" enters imperial tribute, with hundreds of distinct patterns
Ming–Qing
Legends Spread — The tale of weaver Xilan becomes widely known, lending the craft deep humanist meaning

Today

The Making Journey

From Concept to Creation · Six-Step Process

The project took months of meticulous craft — translating traditional brocade patterns into pixel art, then realising them across three distinct forms: handmade bead mosaics, paper-engineered pop-up spreads, and augmented reality. Every step is a dialogue between innovation and tradition.

01
RESEARCH · Field Study

Immersing in Tujia Culture

Systematic collection of traditional Xilankapu patterns, studying their geometric structures and colour logic. Tujia brocade motifs are built on highly abstracted geometry — a quality that maps remarkably well onto pixel art. Every pattern can be understood as a handwoven "pixel matrix."

02
CONCEPT · Pixel Translation

Redrawing Ancient Patterns on a Grid

Using pixel art software, each Xilankapu motif is redrawn grid-square by grid-square. The protagonist Xilan's costume, butterfly totems, phoenix legends, swimming fish, mountain landscapes, and loom scenes are all unified into a consistent pixel visual language. A black-dominant palette stays faithful to the deep-ground tradition of the original brocade.

03
CRAFT · Bead Making

Handcrafted Bead Cover · One Peg at a Time

The cover and bookmark are assembled bead by bead using Hama iron beads. The pixel design is printed as a reference, then coloured beads are placed one at a time onto a pegboard — butterfly motifs, geometric borders, and the title text all realised in beads. Heat from an iron fuses them into a solid, rigid pixel panel.

PRINT & CUT · Printing & Pop-Up Engineering

Interior Pages & Pop-Up Mechanism Design

Story spreads are printed on black card stock, each page featuring a unique pop-up structure: spiral vortexes, pixel mountains, schools of fish, and mythical phoenixes spring up as the page is turned, creating a miniature theatre. A pull-out drawer page reveals two distinct Xilankapu brocade patterns, letting readers experience the motif shift firsthand.

05
BINDING · Book Binding

Leporello Binding · Hardcover Slipcase

The entire book uses a leporello (accordion) binding — it can be fully unfolded into a continuous long panorama, or folded into a compact hardcover slipcase. The bead mosaic cover is embedded in the front board; a red ribbon spine nods to the festive colour of Xilan's legend. Extras include a black-ground illustrated map with pixel icons of story locations, plus a small handmade bead ornament.

AR / DIGITAL · Digital Enhancement

Cover Craft

A Xilankapu Built One Bead at a Time

The cover's key visual is realised through Hama iron-bead craft — butterfly motifs, geometric borders, and the title lettering all recreated with individual 5 mm colour beads. It is the most patience-testing, detail-demanding stage of the entire project.

Hama Iron-Bead Craft

Following the pixel design as a guide, coloured beads are placed one by one onto a white pegboard by colour code, then fused with an iron. The technique translates brocade's thread-based medium into a resin-bead pixel matrix — a material shift from "line" to "dot."

Multiple bead panels progress simultaneously on the workstation, with each colour sorted into its own compartment and tweezers used for precise placement. The heavy use of black and white echoes the deep-ground tradition of original Xilankapu weaving.

Opening Every Page of the Story

Xilan Odyssey follows a young girl named Xilan as she falls into a fantastical world built of pixels and brocade, befriending fish, phoenixes, and weavers, and carrying forward the spirit and craft of Xilankapu. The leporello binding means every page-turn reveals a new popping scene.

Meeting the Coloured Fish

Vivid fish drift slowly through the water, a small shoal swimming toward her. Xilan sets foot on a mountain path and ventures deeper into this strange new world.
